BWINDI IMPENETRABLE NATIONAL PARK

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park isn’t only an iconic location to find the unique mountain gorillas but it is also one destination to experience Africa’s true remoteness, irresistible natural beauty and biodiversity. The park is most popular being home to rare mountain gorillas attracting a significant number of visitors each year who come to enjoy thrilling gorilla trekking experiences.

A face to face encounter with mountain gorillas in the natural tropical forest is by far the most unforgettable wildlife experience. It is a dream come true, spending hours searching for a family of these unique apes and finally, you locate them.

About Bwindi National Park, location, & size

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park –BINP is a premier gorilla trekking park in the Pearl of Africa (Uganda), home to 459 mountain gorillas. The park largely features a lush tropical rainforest, gazetted in 1991 and spans up to 331sq.km. In 1994, Bwindi NP was recognized as a World Heritage Site given its unique biodiversity inhabiting distinct floral and faunal species.

Location

Bwindi is situated in the Southwestern part of Uganda, along the Rift Valley edge. It is one of the parks in Africa where tropical rainforest still exists, deriving its name from Bwindi Impenetrable Forest. The park is bordered by D.R. Congo in the western part and Kabale town borders it in the Southeast, about 29km.

Bwindi lies in Kigezi region rising at an altitude of 1190 to 2607m. Rwamunyonyi Hill is the highest point of Bwindi National Park, and is located in the Eastern sector –Ruhija region.

Attractions in Bwindi National Park

Wildlife/Animals

One of the oldest rainforests in Africa makes up part of dense Bwindi NP, where its diversity of endemic species and other species live. A record of 120 mammal species all reside in this park and include clawless otters, civets, side-striped jackals, giant forest hogs, buffaloes, elephants, black-fronted duikers, golden cats, and more.

About 11 species of primates live in Bwindi and include over 459 mountain gorillas, baboons, chimpanzees, pottos, DeBrazza monkeys, L’hoest monkeys, spectacled Galagos, blue monkeys, red-tailed monkeys, black and white colobus monkeys.

Bird species

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park boasts of more than 360 bird species which comprise of 23 Albertine rift endemic and many forest bird species. The different birds to be spotted on birding tour in Bwindi include White-bellied robin chat, Chapin’s flycatchers, Fraser’s eagle, Handsome francolins, Shelley’s crimson-wing, Western bronze napped pigeon, Regal sunbird, Black billed turacos,

Mountain –masked collared apalis, Grauer’s broadbill, standard winged nightjar, fine-banded woodpeckers, Archer’s robin chat, Montana oriole, Doherty’s bush shrike, African wood owl, Bar-tailed trogon, Kivu ground thrush, black breasted barbet, and others. Mubwindi swamp in Ruhija area of Bwindi NP is one of the best locations for bird watching.

Amphibians & butterflies –plus other species

A total of about 200 species of butterflies (including cream-banded swallowtail and African giant swallowtail), 27 amphibian species live in Bwindi Impenetrable National Park. In addition, there are 14 lizards and snakes thriving within Bwindi Impenetrable Forest.

Tourism Activities to do in Bwindi

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park has plenty of amazing things for every nature enthusiast to enjoy on Uganda safaris. Here are some of the most memorable tourism activities for you to expect to do;

Gorilla trekking

With over 20 habituated gorilla families and 4 sectors, a visit to Bwindi National Park on a gorilla tour guarantees visitors to go trek mountain gorillas. Bwindi hosts the largest population of mountain gorillas, about 459 out of the 1063, making it a preferred destination to locate these apes in their natural habitat. The 4 gorilla sectors to be visited for gorilla trekking in Bwindi include Ruhija, Rushaga, Buhoma and Nkuringo.

We advise all primate enthusiasts to have their gorilla trips to Bwindi Impenetrable National Park planned in advance to avoid the last minute rush. Part of gorilla tour planning involves obtaining a gorilla permit and only persons above 15 years are in position to go track gorillas. All foreign non-residents pay USD 700 for a gorilla permit, foreign residents USD 600 for foreign residents and all East African citizens require shs. 250,000.

A normal gorilla trek begins at 8:00 am after visitors have been briefed and allocated gorilla families. The search for mountain gorillas takes long, about 2-6 hours with visitors guaranteed of one hour for a close encounter.

The entire gorilla trek requires adequate preparation and includes packing right. Only essentials are needed to make your gorilla tour successful and your packing list should include waterproof daypack, hiking boots, rain jacket, enough bottled drinking water, a pair of long trousers, a camera free of flashlight, a pair of socks, long-sleeved shirts, binoculars, garden gloves, safari hat, and others.

Birding

Birding is a special experience worth enjoying by any enthusiastic birder on Uganda safari in Bwindi NP. Bwindi is one of the best bird watching areas and hosts over 360 bird species. The birds to expect to identify while in Bwindi Impenetrable National Park include Kivu ground thrush, Chapin’s flycatchers, Black billed turacos, Grauer’s broadbill, Fraser’s eagle, Regal sunbird, Purple breasted sunbird, mountain-masked apalis, western bronze napped pigeon, and others. The must-visit birding areas in this park include Mubwindi Swamp in Ruhija sector.

Nature walks

A visit to Bwindi is beyond trekking mountain gorillas. If you are an enthusiastic nature traveler or hiker, then Bwindi N/Park is a special place you shouldn’t miss to visit. The park has many hiking/nature walking opportunities and each hiking trail offers unique experiences. They include Habinyanja trail, bamboo trail, waterfall, River Ivy trail, Muzubijiro, Kashasha river trail, and others.

Community tours

Community/cultural walks introduce visitors to the nearby local communities to interact with local residents. The must-visit communities adjacent to the park include Bakiga and Batwa pygmies’ communities. The Batwa are the ancient inhabitants of Bwindi Impenetrable Forest and left when it was gazetted as a park in 1991. They are best referred to as forest keepers, hunters and gatherers. Regardless of being hunter-gatherers, the Batwa lived harmoniously with mountain gorillas and other wildlife.

Other areas to consider for community tours include Nyundo Community Eco trails, Aquaponics projects, and King Bakyara waterfall trail.

Best time to visit the park

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park is visited all year-round for various reasons besides gorilla trekking experiences. If you would like to have less-stressing adventures in this park, then your trip should start during the dry season: June to September or December to February.

These are the months of the year when the park records little or no rainfall with the skies clear, while the ground is kept drier making it easier walking in the forest as you look for your gorilla family. During the wet season, there is heavy rainfall and trekking through the park is a bit challenging due to muddy ground and dense vegetation cover. But they come with plenty of forage for gorillas to feed on and often, visitors spend few hours searching for these primates.
