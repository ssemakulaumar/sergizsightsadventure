MURCHISON FALLS NATIONAL PARK

If you are looking for one of the most attractive destinations to explore in Africa, then Murchison Falls National Park -(MFNP) should be a must-visit. Murchison NP takes its name from the spectacular Murchison Falls and offers true African wilderness experiences. This park is among the many protected areas that are managed by Uganda Wildlife Authority (UWA). Murchison Falls N/Park has its boundaries extend covering parts of Lake Albert, and surrounding districts include Kiryandongo, Masindi, Nwoya and Buliisa.

MFNP in Uganda was founded as a park in 1952 and originally, it was referred to as Kabalega National Park. This park covers up to 3840 sq.km and features other reserves including Budongo Forest Reserve, Bunyoro Game Reserve, and Karuma Wildlife Reserve.

Location

Murchison Falls NP is found in Northwestern Uganda. From the heart of Kampala to Murchison Falls National Park, it is about 4-5 hours drive. This park boasts of its epic landscape and diverse habitat where its abundant wildlife live.

Main Attractions in Murchison Falls N/Park in Uganda

Murchison Falls

The Murchison Falls is amazingly the iconic attraction in Murchison Falls N. Park in Uganda. The park is separated into two by the Victoria Nile River forming a powerful Murchison Falls that forces itself into a narrow rock of 8m, before cascading about 45m creating devils cauldron and permanent rainbow. There are two options to get to the top of the falls: one you can take a boat up to the base and disembark then begin hiking 45 minutes or drive direct to the top of the falls for your photography excursion.

Wildlife

Murchison Falls Park in Uganda prides itself of abundant wildlife and accounts for 76 mammal species, 51 reptiles, 451 bird species, 51 amphibian species and others. The numerous wildlife species to be encountered on Uganda safaris in Murchison Falls N. Park include the cape buffaloes, lions, elephants, Rothschild giraffes, hippos, crocodiles, Jacksons hartebeest, leopards, Uganda kobs, oribis, and more.

Birds

Over 451 bird species all reside in Murchison Falls Park. They different avifaunal species in this park include Abyssinian ground hornbill, grey crowned crane, black headed lapwing, Senegal thick knee, Denhams bustards, Standard winged nightjars, long-toed lapwings, long-tailed nightjars, squacco herons, black billed barbet, rock pratincole, long tailed plovers, white browed sparrow weavers, saddle billed storks, sandpipers, malachite kingfishers, palm nut vultures, yellow fronted tinker birds, giant kingfishers, martial eagle, African qualifinch, African jacanas, giant kingfishers, red throated bee-eaters, red throated bee-eaters, and others.

In Budongo Forest, the Southern side of Murchison Falls National Park, possible birds to be spotted include blue breasted kingfishers, chocolate backed kingfishers, lemon bellied crombec, crowned eagle, African dwarf kingfishers, yellow footed flycatchers, Cassins hawk eagle, black eared ground thrush, grey headed sunbird, little green sunbird, chestnut capped flycatchers, brown twin spot, Puvel’s illadopsis, Cassins spine tail, olive breasted greenbul, and others.

Lake Albert delta –birds to expect to identify around this area include warblers, kingfishers, shoebill storks, woodpeckers, malachite kingfishers, ducks, herons and others. These can be spotted on boat cruise along the delta.

Tourism Activities

Game drives

Murchison Falls N. Park in Uganda is by far a preferred wildlife park, thanks to its distinct safari game drives, most enjoyable along its golden savanna grasslands and riverine woodland of Buligi peninsula and other game tracks. A game drive in this park is a great opportunity to encounter abundant wildlife including African bush elephants, lions, leopards, endangered Rothschild giraffes, warthogs, leopards, hyenas, and more. You can opt to engage in the morning or afternoon guided game drive sessions.

Boat cruise

Boat cruise excursions are some of the signature experiences that await tourists on Uganda safaris to Murchison Falls National Park. There are 2 options to explore and experience the park on a boat cruise –you can go on a boat cruise along the Victoria Nile to the base of the falls or take Lake Albert delta boat ride. The beauty about this water sport is that nature enthusiasts are guaranteed of incredible sightings of aquatic species such as hippos, crocodiles and water birds.

Chimpanzee tracking

Chimpanzee tracking offers visitors an incredible chance to enjoy a close view of chimpanzees while in their natural habitat. Chimpanzees and most primates reside in Budongo Forest, the Southern side of Murchison Falls Park. The 2-4 hours chimpanzee trek involves immersing yourself into the jungles of Budongo/Kaniyo-Pabidi forest and you also stand a chance to encounter diversity of other primates.

Hot air balloon

Hot air balloon tours allow you enjoy wildlife encounter in a different way from normal game drives. Balloon tours offer typically aerial game viewing experiences and you can capture aerial pictures of savanna dwellers and breathtaking landscape of Murchison Falls NP.

Birding

Murchison Falls N/Park is a rich birding tour destination, home to all 451 bird species and includes endemic species. The popular birds to be spotted on bird walking tour in this park include palm nut vultures, Denham’s bustards, black billed barbets, African jacanas, Abyssinian ground hornbills, malachite kingfishers, Eastern grey plantain eaters, black headed lapwing, red throated bee-eaters, speckle fronted weavers, shoebill storks, giant kingfishers, squacco herons, silver bird, and others.

Sport fishing

Sport fishing is a must-do water sport on Murchison Falls safaris. This is done at the base of Murchison Falls and at Karuma Falls. Anglers get a chance to catch heavy Nile perch and other fish species using their fishing gear. Foreign nonresidents and foreign residents pay USD 50 per day for sport fishing.

White water rafting

This is a newly launched experience and it is conducted along the Victoria Nile River in Murchison Falls NP. Visitors are charged USD 50 for this adventure –if you are foreign nonresident or foreign resident and East Africa citizens pay Uganda shs. 50,000.

Best time to visit

Murchison Falls NP is an all year-round wildlife safari destination, and experiences annual rainfall record of 1085mm, especially around March and November. The favorable months of the year to visit the park are June, July, August to September and December, January to February –best months of the year when there is low or no rainfall.

Accommodation –budget, midrange and luxury safari lodges

Luxury lodges: Chobe Safari Lodge, Baker’s Lodge, Paraa Safari Lodge, Nile Safari Lodge, etc. Midrange lodges- Fort Murchison, Pakuba Safari Lodge, Murchison River Lodge, and budget options include Red Chili Rest Camp, Heritage Lodge, Sambiya River Lodge, Boomu Bandas and others.

How to get there

Murchison Falls National Park is comprised of gates such as Chobe, Tangi, Wankar, Kichumbanyobo, and Mubako gates. The park is accessible by road and air and by road; you can spend 5-6 hours to get to there from Kampala-Gulu highway. Or take charter/scheduled flights setting off from Entebbe Airport to Chobe, Bugungu and Pakuba airstrips in Murchison Falls NP.
